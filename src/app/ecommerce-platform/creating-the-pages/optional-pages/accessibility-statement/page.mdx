# Accessibility Statement

## What is an Accessibility Statement

An Accessibility Statement is a public declaration that outlines how a website or application meets accessibility standards and guidelines. It provides information about the measures taken to ensure that the platform is usable by individuals with disabilities, including those with visual, auditory, motor, or cognitive impairments.

## Why is an Accessibility Statement Important

An Accessibility Statement is important because it:

- Demonstrates Commitment: It shows that your business is committed to inclusivity and accessibility for all users, regardless of their abilities.
- Builds Trust: It helps build trust with users by providing transparency about your accessibility efforts and the standards you adhere to.
- Ensures Compliance: Many jurisdictions require businesses to comply with accessibility standards, such as the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) or the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA). An Accessibility Statement can help demonstrate compliance with these regulations.
- Provides Information: It informs users about the accessibility features of your platform, how to use them, and how to report any accessibility issues they may encounter.

## Key Components of an Accessibility Statement

A comprehensive Accessibility Statement should include the following key components:

- Accessibility Standards: Specify the accessibility standards your platform adheres to, such as WCAG 2.1 or WCAG 2.2, and the level of conformance (e.g., Level AA).
- Accessibility Features: Describe the accessibility features available on your platform, such as keyboard navigation, screen reader compatibility, text resizing options, and color contrast adjustments.
- Testing and Evaluation: Outline the methods used to test and evaluate the accessibility of your platform, including any third-party audits or user testing with individuals with disabilities.
- Feedback Mechanism:
  Provide a way for users to report accessibility issues or provide feedback on the accessibility of your platform.
- Contact Information: Include contact details for users to reach out with questions or concerns about accessibility.
- Continuous Improvement: State your commitment to continuously improving the accessibility of your platform and how you plan to achieve this, such as regular audits, user feedback, and updates to accessibility features.
- Date of Last Update: Indicate when the Accessibility Statement was last reviewed or updated to ensure users have the most current information.
- Legal Disclaimer: Include any legal disclaimers regarding the accessibility of your platform, such as limitations or exceptions to accessibility features.
- Resources: Provide links to additional resources or guidelines related to web accessibility, such as WCAG documentation or government accessibility standards.

## Implementing an Accessibility Statement

To implement an Accessibility Statement on your e-commerce platform, follow these steps:

1. **Draft the Statement**: Create a clear and concise Accessibility Statement that covers all the key components mentioned above. You can use online templates or consult with accessibility experts to ensure compliance with applicable standards.
2. **Make it Accessible**: Add a link to the Accessibility Statement in the footer of your website, ensuring it is easily accessible from all pages. You can also include a link during the account creation process or checkout to ensure users are aware of it before using your service.
3. **Obtain Feedback**: Encourage users to provide feedback on the accessibility of your platform and any issues they may encounter. This can be done through a dedicated feedback form or contact email.
4. **Review and Update**: Regularly review and update your Accessibility Statement to reflect any changes in your platform, accessibility features, or compliance with new standards. Notify users of any significant changes and provide them with the opportunity to review the updated statement.
5. **Train Your Team**: Ensure that your team members are familiar with the Accessibility Statement and understand how to handle user interactions in compliance with it. This includes customer support representatives, marketing teams, and developers who may interact with users.
6. **Monitor Compliance**: Regularly monitor your compliance with the Accessibility Statement and applicable accessibility standards. This may involve conducting audits, reviewing accessibility features, and addressing any potential issues promptly.
7. **Seek Expert Advice**: If you have any doubts or concerns about your Accessibility Statement or compliance with accessibility standards, consider seeking advice from accessibility experts or legal professionals who specialize in web accessibility. They can help ensure that your statement is comprehensive and compliant with relevant laws and standards.

## Example 2: Comprehensive Government/Educational Institution Style

##### NOTE

> This example follows a more formal structure often used by government agencies and educational institutions, with detailed conformance information and specific testing procedures.

```txt
ACCESSIBILITY STATEMENT FOR [ORGANIZATION NAME]

Effective Date: June 27, 2025
Last Reviewed: June 27, 2025

COMMITMENT TO ACCESSIBILITY

[Organization Name] is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people with disabilities. We are continually improving the user experience for everyone and applying the relevant accessibility standards.

CONFORMANCE STATUS

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) define requirements for designers and developers to improve accessibility for people with disabilities. It defines three levels of conformance: Level A, Level AA, and Level AAA.

This website is partially conformant with WCAG 2.1 Level AA. "Partially conformant" means that some parts of the content do not fully conform to the accessibility standard.

CURRENT ACCESSIBILITY FEATURES

Our website includes the following accessibility features:

VISUAL ACCESSIBILITY
- High contrast color schemes available
- Text can be resized up to 200% without loss of functionality
- Images include meaningful alternative text descriptions
- Videos include captions and audio descriptions where applicable
- Focus indicators are clearly visible for keyboard navigation

MOTOR ACCESSIBILITY
- All functionality is available via keyboard navigation
- Sufficient target size for interactive elements (minimum 44x44 pixels)
- No time limits on essential functions unless necessary for security
- Content does not flash more than three times per second

AUDITORY ACCESSIBILITY
- Captions provided for all video content with audio
- Audio descriptions available for visual content in videos
- No background audio plays automatically
- Visual alternatives provided for audio-only content

COGNITIVE ACCESSIBILITY
- Clear and consistent navigation throughout the site
- Simple language used where possible, with explanations for complex terms
- Error messages are descriptive and provide suggestions for correction
- Multiple ways to find content (search, site map, navigation)

TESTING METHODOLOGY

We employ multiple testing methods to ensure accessibility:

AUTOMATED TESTING
- Regular scans using axe-core accessibility engine
- Lighthouse accessibility audits on all major pages
- Pa11y command-line testing integrated into our development workflow

MANUAL TESTING
- Keyboard-only navigation testing
- Screen reader testing with NVDA, JAWS, and VoiceOver
- Color contrast verification using WebAIM's Contrast Checker
- User testing with individuals who have various disabilities

THIRD-PARTY AUDITING
- Annual accessibility audits conducted by certified accessibility professionals
- Quarterly reviews by our internal accessibility team
- Ongoing monitoring through automated accessibility monitoring tools

KNOWN ISSUES AND LIMITATIONS

We are aware of the following accessibility limitations and are working to address them:

- Some legacy PDF documents may not be fully accessible; we are working to update these
- Third-party embedded content (social media widgets, maps) may not meet our accessibility standards
- Some complex data visualizations are still being enhanced for screen reader compatibility

FEEDBACK AND COMPLAINTS PROCESS

We welcome feedback on the accessibility of our website. If you encounter accessibility barriers, please contact us using one of the following methods:

PRIMARY CONTACT
Email: accessibility@organization.org
Phone: +1 (555) 987-6543 (Voice/TTY available)
Online Form: [Link to accessible feedback form]

POSTAL ADDRESS
Accessibility Coordinator
[Organization Name]
456 Accessibility Way
Suite 200
Portland, OR 97205

RESPONSE TIMEFRAME
We aim to respond to accessibility feedback within 3 business days and resolve issues within 10 business days when possible.

ALTERNATIVE FORMATS

If you need information from our website in an alternative format, such as large print, Braille, or audio recording, please contact us using the information above. We will work with you to provide the information in a format that meets your needs.

ENFORCEMENT AND LEGAL COMPLIANCE

This accessibility statement is created in accordance with:
- Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act
- Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) Title II
- WCAG 2.1 Level AA guidelines

If you are not satisfied with our response to your accessibility concern, you may file a complaint with:
- Our internal accessibility ombudsman at ombudsman@organization.org
- The U.S. Department of Justice Civil Rights Division
- Your local disability rights organization

CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT

We are committed to ongoing improvement of our digital accessibility. Our efforts include:
- Regular staff training on accessibility best practices
- Incorporating accessibility reviews into our content creation process
- Updating our accessibility policies and procedures annually
- Participating in accessibility conferences and professional development

This statement will be updated as we make improvements to our website accessibility.
```
